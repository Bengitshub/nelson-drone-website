Good to know

Questions people ask

Straight answers — the legal side, the practical side, and how booking works.

Do you need a licence to fly a drone commercially in New Zealand?

We operate under the Civil Aviation Authority's Part 101 rules, flown safely and legally every time. That covers commercial video and photography — no separate pilot licence required. For anything beyond standard limits, we arrange the right authorisation first.

Can you legally fly in Nelson city and near the airport?

Much of Nelson, Richmond and the Waimea plains sit under Nelson Airport's controlled airspace. We take care of the air-traffic authorisation (via AirShare) or use low-level "shielded" flying where it applies — so the airspace paperwork is handled before we ever turn up.

What areas do you cover?

Nelson, Richmond, Motueka, the wider Tasman district and Marlborough. Got a spot further out? Ask — we travel for the right project.

Can you film indoors?

Yes — that's an FPV speciality. Guarded props let us fly safely through doorways, rooms and tight spaces to get the seamless interior fly-through a normal camera drone can't.

What happens if the weather turns?

Drones don't fly in strong wind or rain, so if conditions aren't safe we simply reschedule at no charge. Nelson gives us plenty of good-weather days to work with.

How much does it cost, and how do I pay?

Every job is quoted to your location and what you need, so get in touch for a quick price. Booking is simple: we confirm the date, do the shoot, then email your invoice afterwards. No online payment to wrestle with.
