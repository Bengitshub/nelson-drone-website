The Weather Promise

If the light's wrong, I ring you first.

Drone work lives and dies on the weather, so I treat the forecast as part of the job. I watch it from the moment you book, and if the window isn't going to give you good material, I call it early — usually the day before — and we pick the next one.

A weather move never costs you anything, and it's never treated as a cancellation. You booked pictures worth paying for, not a drone in the air on a grey day.

What that means in practice

  • Reschedules are free, always
  • The call comes the day before, not an hour after
  • The pilot's safety call is final — no pressure flying
  • If I can't deliver the shot list, you don't pay for it

The pilot

One person. The whole job.

Haven Aerials is me — one pilot planning, flying, editing and delivering every job. You brief the person who pushes the sticks, and the person who edits your files is the one who framed them.

I fly a DJI Avata 360: small enough to work close, agile enough for fly-throughs, and its 360 capture means I can pull new angles out of a flight after it's landed. I fly to New Zealand's drone rules, and if a site can't be flown legally, I'll tell you before you spend a cent.

Good to know

Fair questions

Is checking a date a commitment?
No — it sends me a request and holds the slot. The booking is only confirmed after I've checked the site and you've accepted the written quote.
What arrives at the end?
A download link, organised into folders: full-resolution stills, web-sized copies, and any clips or edits from your package. Photos land within three working days, video within five.
What happens when the weather turns bad?
I call it early and we move to the next good window, free. See the Weather Promise above — it's the way this business runs.
Can you fly my location?
Probably, but I check first. Airspace near Nelson Airport, people, neighbouring properties and wildlife all shape the plan — that's the homework I do before quoting.
How does payment work?
After your files are delivered I send a Stripe invoice — pay online by card or bank transfer. Nothing is charged through this website.
Do you do weddings or big productions?
Not as full films. I can capture aerial photos or clips of a venue or outdoor event, but I'm one pilot, not a production crew — and I'd rather say that up front.

Got a spot in mind?
