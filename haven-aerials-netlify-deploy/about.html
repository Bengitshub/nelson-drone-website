The operation

Small on purpose.

Haven Aerials is named for Nelson Haven — the sheltered stretch of water tucked behind the Boulder Bank, calm while the open coast does its thing. That's the feeling I want every job to have: protected, planned, no nasty surprises.

Being one person is the point. When you brief me, you're briefing the person who frames the shot. When you get your files back, they were edited by the same person who flew them. Nothing gets lost in a handover, because there isn't one.

The kit

What I fly

The aircraft is a DJI Avata 360. It's small and agile, which means it works close to buildings and through spaces a bigger drone can't. It flies smooth fly-throughs, and its 360 capture lets me pull several different angles out of a single flight after it's landed — useful when a site only gives me one good weather window.

It's the right tool for most of what I'm asked to do, but it isn't the right tool for everything. If your job would be better served by a different camera platform, I'll tell you that before I quote — not after you've paid.

Before takeoff

The homework I do on every job

  • Airspace. Much of Nelson sits under the airport control zone. I check what's needed and get clearance before I plan to fly.
  • Permission. I confirm who owns and manages the site, and that there's permission to be there and overhead.
  • People. I plan the flight path around who'll be on and around the site, and how to keep clear of them.
  • Wildlife and privacy. Nesting birds, marine mammals and the neighbours all shape where and when I fly.
  • The weather window. I watch the forecast from the day you book — that's the Weather Promise, and it's the whole reason I call marginal days early.

I fly under New Zealand's standard drone rules — Civil Aviation Authority Part 101. In plain terms that means keeping the aircraft in sight, staying within the height limit, getting consent before flying over people and property, and holding clearance in controlled airspace near Nelson Airport. Those are obligations I meet on every flight. If a location can't be flown legally and safely, it doesn't get flown — and I'll say so before you spend anything.
