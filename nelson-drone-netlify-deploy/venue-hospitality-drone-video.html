One shot, straight through your venue

A venue flythrough is a single continuous take — no cuts, no clips stitched together to fake the motion. We fly exterior to interior in one move: through the doorway, past the bar, threading between tables and out the other side. That unbroken flow is what a normal camera drone can't do, and it's exactly what keeps people watching to the end.

Sells the feeling, not just the fit-out

People pick a restaurant, café or cellar door on a feeling long before they read the menu. A flythrough captures that momentum — the space, the light, the energy of a full room — and puts a customer inside it before they've booked. It works just as hard for a gym floor, a function room or a hotel lobby.

Yes, we can fly it indoors — safely

Flying inside is an FPV speciality, not a workaround. Guarded props let us move safely through doorways, around guests and into tight corners, so the one take keeps running from outside to in. That's how you get a seamless interior tour instead of a slideshow of separate angles. We walk the space and plan the line before anything leaves the ground.

Made to stop the scroll

A restaurant drone tour earns its keep on social. We cut the flythrough for vertical feeds — Instagram, TikTok and Facebook — so it lands as content people share, not an ad they skip past. One shoot can feed your reels, your website hero and your Google Business Profile.

Airspace and the legal side, sorted

Much of Nelson and the Waimea plains sit under the airport's controlled airspace, so most shoots need authorisation before anyone flies. We handle the AirShare approval or use low-level shielded flying where it applies. We operate under CAA Part 101 with a pilot and a spotter on site — and if the weather turns, we reschedule at no charge.

Booking is refreshingly low-tech

No apps, no online checkout to wrestle with. Enquire or give us a call, we confirm the date and quote your job, we shoot, then the invoice arrives by email once you've seen the film. Every job is priced to your venue and what you need, so get in touch for a quote.

FAQ

Questions & answers

Can you really fly a drone inside my restaurant or cellar door?

Yes — it's what FPV does best. With guarded props we fly safely through doorways, around furniture and between people, keeping the shot running from outside to in. We walk the space and plan the flight line before we fly a metre.

Do we need to close the venue for the shoot?

Not necessarily. A quiet period or a lightly staged setup usually gives the cleanest run, but we can plan around your hours or capture a genuine busy buzz if that's the look you want. We'll sort the plan when we confirm the booking.

How much does a venue flythrough cost?

Every job is quoted to your location, the size of the space and how many social cuts you need. Get in touch and we'll send a quick price — the invoice only comes after the shoot, by email. No online payment on the website.

What areas do you cover?

Nelson, Richmond, Motueka, the wider Tasman district and Marlborough. If your venue is a bit further out, ask — we travel for the right project.
