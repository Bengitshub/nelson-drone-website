About Skyframe

Meet the Nelson drone operator behind Skyframe

Why FPV, and why it matters to your film

Standard camera drones hover and glide — lovely, but always at a polite distance. FPV is flown manually, like it's on rails, so it can dive, chase, bank and thread through tight spaces in one unbroken move. That single continuous take is the shot people actually watch to the end. It's harder to fly and it takes real practice, which is exactly why it stands out from everything else in the feed.

  • One continuous exterior-to-interior fly-through
  • Dive shots, dynamic reveals and proximity flying
  • Fast chase footage — bikes, boats, kayaks, runners
  • The shot a normal hovering drone simply can't get

The gear we fly

Skyframe flies a DJI Avata 2-class FPV drone — a compact, agile aircraft with ducted, guarded props. That prop guarding is what lets it fly safely close to people and through interiors, where a large open-prop drone can't go. It's light, quick and built for exactly the kind of tight, immersive work we specialise in — indoors and out.

Flown safe, flown legal — every flight

Every shoot runs by the book. We operate under the Civil Aviation Authority's Part 101 rules — the framework that lets us fly commercially without a separate pilot licence — and we carry insurance. Because goggled FPV means the pilot's view is inside the headset, every job runs with a dedicated spotter watching the aircraft and the surroundings. And with much of Nelson under airport-controlled airspace, we sort the AirShare authorisation, or use shielded low-level flying, before we ever arrive.

  • CAA Part 101 compliant — no pilot licence needed to trade
  • Fully insured
  • Pilot plus spotter on every FPV shoot
  • Airspace authorisations and consents handled for you
  • Free reschedule if wind or rain makes it unsafe

Genuinely local to Nelson and Tasman

We're not flying in from out of town. Skyframe is based in the Nelson–Tasman region and knows the ground here — the light on the Waimea plains, the coast out toward Kaiteriteri, the vineyards through to Marlborough, and where the airspace lines fall. That local knowledge means less guesswork, smarter shot planning and a crew that turns up already knowing the lie of the land. We cover Nelson, Richmond, Motueka, the wider Tasman district and Marlborough — and travel further for the right project.

FAQ

Questions & answers

Are you a licensed drone operator in New Zealand?

We operate under CAA Part 101 — the rules that allow commercial drone video and photography without a separate pilot licence. For anything beyond standard limits, we arrange the right authorisation first.

Do you fly with a spotter?

Always, for FPV work. Flying in goggles means the pilot's view is inside the headset, so a dedicated spotter keeps eyes on the drone and the surroundings the whole time.

What drone do you use?

A DJI Avata 2-class FPV drone with ducted, guarded props — small, agile and safe to fly close to people and indoors.

Are you actually based in Nelson?

Yes. Skyframe is local to the Nelson–Tasman region and covers Nelson, Richmond, Motueka, the wider Tasman district and Marlborough.
