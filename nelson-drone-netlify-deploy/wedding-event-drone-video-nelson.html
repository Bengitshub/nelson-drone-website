We sort the airspace and the paperwork

Much of Nelson, Richmond and the Waimea plains sit under Nelson Airport's controlled airspace, and plenty of great venues fall inside it. That's our job to handle, not yours. We arrange the air-traffic authorisation through AirShare or use low-level shielded flying where it applies, all sorted well before the day. We operate under the Civil Aviation Authority's Part 101 rules, flown safely and legally every time — no licence hoops for you to jump through.

Summer dates and the weather plan

Summer is peak season for Nelson and Tasman weddings, and dates book out — so if you've got a warm-weather wedding in mind, get in touch early to lock the calendar. Drones don't fly safely in strong wind or heavy rain, and a wedding date can't move, so we plan around the forecast and fly in the calm windows through your day. For separate engagement or event shoots that can shift, we simply reschedule at no charge if conditions turn.

FAQ

Questions & answers

Won't a drone be noisy and disruptive during the ceremony?

We plan the close, dynamic passes for the big open moments — the entrance, the vows, the confetti and the dance floor — not the quiet parts. The flight plan is built around your run sheet, and with a spotter on the ground we keep it safe and unobtrusive. The drone should show up in the film, not get in the way of your day.

Our venue is near Nelson Airport. Can you still fly?

Almost certainly, yes. A lot of great Nelson and Richmond venues sit under controlled airspace, and handling that is our job. We arrange the authorisation through AirShare or use shielded low-level flying where it applies, all sorted before we arrive.

What happens if it's windy or wet on the wedding day?

Your date can't move, so we watch the forecast closely and fly in the calmer windows through the day. If a passing squall grounds us for a bit, we work around it and get the shots when it's safe. We'll never put the drone, or your guests, at risk for a shot.

Do you cover weddings and events outside Nelson city?

Yes — Nelson, Richmond, Motueka, the wider Tasman district and Marlborough are all home turf. Getting married somewhere further out? Ask us; we travel for the right wedding.

How do we book and pay?

It's simple and non-tech. Get in touch with your date and venue, we confirm availability and send a quote, then shoot the day. The invoice comes by email afterwards via Stripe — there's no online payment to wrestle with on the website.
